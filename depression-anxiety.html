Depression & Anxiety

Depression is a prison where you are both the suffering prisoner and the cruel jailer.

- Dorothy Rowe

Depression

Depression is an abnormal exhibition of a lessened mood affecting how you feel and think and in turn to that how you behave as a person. According to the world health organization, approximately 4.4 percent of the world’s population lives with depression.

Sadness is a reaction due to the outcome of depression. The difference between sadness and other emotions is that the person avoids taking any sort of action. This causes the person to go into endless thought loops which in the long run does more harm rather than give a solution.

The brain is prone to think that there isn’t a productive solution and leaves the person hopeless, ultimately putting the person in a depressive state.

    Some of the most common symptoms of depression include:

  • Poor concentration
  • Thoughts of harming yourself
  • Feeling of worthlessness
  • Lack of energy
  • Low moods

Depression is an old enemy of human motivation and can have serious effects on a person’s mental health, their social life and their productivity.

    The Four different types of depression include:

  • Clinical Depression
  • Bipolar Depression
  • Situational Depression
  • Psychotic Depression
  • Low moods

Depression can be quite draining and often leaves the person feeling tired and empty. The best approach to handle depression is by first understanding why is occurs and how your current thought process is affecting your energy levels and secondly by being open and accepting towards yourself and the situation you are going through.

There are a number of ways depression can be countered with:

Understanding how the mind functions

What are the thoughts that lead towards you feeling low about yourself, identify them and confront them with a better approach.

Meditation

Meditation can help calm the mind and allow the person to analyze their thoughts through a different perspective

Socializing

Being in the company of people that care about you can hugely impact a person’s self-worth and can allow the person to talk and discuss matters with others who might be going through similar problems.

Anxiety

Anxiety is the phenomenon where the brain sends signals to the body as if it is in danger however there is no real danger. People suffering from anxiety experience symptoms of stomach aches, shortness of breath and increased heart rate which further increases anxiousness.

Anxiety is quite normal amongst humans and learning about anxiety and recognizing it is the first step towards managing it.

    There are four different types of anxiety disorders, they include:

  • Generalized anxiety disorder
  • Panic disorder
  • Phobias
  • Social anxiety

Anxiety is an adaptive feature of our bodies that allows us to deal with situations that are actually dangerous. It only becomes a problem when the situation is not actually dangerous Anxiety however does not have any harmful effects of you, other than the fact that it makes the individual quite uncomfortable

Anxiety is temporary and does not last forever and gradually will decrease.

    Some of the most common symptoms of anxiety are:

  • Sweating
  • Nausea
  • Dizziness
  • Tightness in chest
  • Tingling sensations
  • Numbness

How Can Anxiety Be Overcome?

Facing your fears

Learning more about yourself and your fears can be an effective way of tackling anxiety

Exercise

Exercise is known to increase levels of serotonin and dopamine in the brain which can increase feelings of happiness and satisfaction which in turn keeps the mind away from thinking of fear.

Meditation

Learning to calm the mind and body, through meditation and other visualization techniques can help overcome feelings of fear

Cognitive behavioral therapy

This is perhaps the most effective way of dealing with anxiety. With CBT you learn to identify and challenge your fearful thoughts patterns and replace them with positive more realistic ones.
